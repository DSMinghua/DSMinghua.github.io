Minghua Chen – Suggestions to Graduate Students

I keep a list of advice that I found useful previously as a graduate student and now as a researcher.

How to do well in graduate study and research:

  • How to succeed in graduate school? By Marie desJardins. It has a chinese version.

  • Does one have to be a genius to do math? By Terry Tao.

  • How to have a bad career in research/academia? by David Patterson.

How to read a paper:

  • How to read a paper? By Srinivasan Keshav.

How to review a paper:

  • In short, the paper-review job is to summarize your professional observations of the paper into four parts: summary, strength, weakness, and detailed comments.

  • Writing reviews for systems conferences. By Timothy Roscoe.

  • How to Review a Paper. By Hugh Davis.

  • How to read and evaluate a scientific paper? By William G. Griswold.

How to write a paper:

  • Write A Rapid Prototype First By Terry Tao.

  • Ten Simple Rules for Mathematical Writing By Dimitri Bertsekas.

  • How to write a great research paper? By Simon Peyton Jones. Here is the corresponding video lecture.

  • Writing Technical Articles By Henning Schulzrinne. (A nice guideline)

How to give a talk:

  • Small Guide to Giving Presentations by Markus Puschel.

  • How to give a good research talk? By Simon L Peyton Jones, John Hughes, and John Launchbury.

  • How to give an academic talk? By Paul N. Edwards.

How to prepare a good poster and poster presentation:

  • How to make a good poster. By LiLynn Graves.

  • A guide to a successful scientific poster presentation. By Susan Koskinen.

  • Do’s and Don’ts of Poster Presentation. By Steven M. Block.

  • How to make a successful poster?

  • “I am the master of my fate: I am the captain of my soul.” – William Ernest Henley (from his poem “Invictus”)
